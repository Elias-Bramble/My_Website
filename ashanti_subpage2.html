CULTURAL GROUPS

The Asante people are part of the broader Akan ethnic group, which is indigenous to the forested region of present-day Ghana in West Africa. The specific origins of the Asante people are believed to trace back to the migration and settlement of Akan-speaking groups in the region around the 15th century. As part of this broader Akan migration, various subgroups, including the Asante, established themselves in the area that would later become the Asante Empire.

The Asante people originally settled in small states and chiefdoms within the forested region of Ghana. Over time, these states gradually coalesced into a unified political entity under the leadership of the Asantehene, or king, leading to the formation of the Asante Empire. The Asante people's cultural and linguistic connections to other Akan groups, as well as their shared traditions and beliefs, are integral to their identity and heritage.
